## Projects

### 1. Superstore Sales & Profitability Analysis

**Dataset:** Sample Superstore (US retail) | **Rows:** 9,994 transactions | **Period:** FY 2014-2017

**Tools:** Microsoft Excel (PivotTables, Charts, Conditional Formatting, Formulas)

#### What I Built

| Sheet | Description |
|---|---|
| Dashboard | KPI tiles + 4 charts (category revenue, annual trend, regional bar, segment margin) |
| PT_Category_Analysis | Sales, profit & margin by product category |
| PT_Subcategory_Analysis | 17 sub-categories ranked by profit with green/red conditional formatting |
| PT_Regional_Analysis | Revenue and margin by US region |
| PT_Segment_Analysis | Consumer / Corporate / Home Office breakdown |
| PT_Yearly_Trends | YoY revenue growth with live Excel formulas |
| PT_Discount_Impact | Discount band analysis revealing margin destruction at 30%+ discounts |
| Insights | 5 structured business insights (Observation > Root Cause > Implication > Metric) |

#### Key Findings
- **Furniture is a margin trap:** 2.5% profit margin vs 12.5% company average — Tables and Bookcases are loss-making sub-categories
- **Discounting above 30% destroys profitability:** The 51%+ discount band yields a -119% margin, wiping out $76.6K in profit
- **West region outperforms:** 14.9% margin vs Central region's 7.9% — a 7pp gap worth investigating
- **Office Supplies is the highest-margin category at 17.0%**, driven by Paper (43.4%) and Labels (44.4%)

### 2. Apparel Retail Basket Analysis

**Dataset:** Apparel basket dataset | **Rows:** 7,660 line items | **Transactions:** 3,000 | **Period:** FY 2025

**Tools:** Microsoft Excel (PivotTables, Charts, Market Basket Analysis, Customer Segmentation)

#### What I Built

| Sheet | Description |
|---|---|
| Dashboard | KPI tiles + 4 charts (product revenue, monthly trend, quarterly, basket distribution) |
| Transaction_Data | Cleaned and formatted raw data with auto-filter + freeze panes |
| PT_Product_Performance | All 15 products ranked by revenue with revenue share % |
| PT_Monthly_Trends | Monthly revenue + live MoM growth % formulas |
| PT_Quarterly_Summary | Quarterly performance with avg basket value per quarter |
| PT_Basket_Analysis | Basket size distribution + top 20 product co-occurrence pairs |
| PT_Customer_Segments | Low/Mid/High value tiers by spend + top 10 customers |
| Insights | 5 structured business insights with quantified implications |

#### Key Findings
- **Jackets + Sneakers drive 33.2% of total revenue** despite being only 2 of 15 products — high unit price, not volume
- **18% of transactions are single-item** — converting 10% of single-item buyers to two-item baskets could yield ~$6,400 incremental revenue
- **Jeans + Shorts (102 co-occurrences)** is the top product pair — a clear "casual bottoms" cluster signals bundle promotion opportunity
- **Revenue is remarkably stable** ($80K-$103K/month) — seasonal product diversification creates a natural smoothing effect
- **High-value customers (top tertile) average $2,378 spend** — 4.3x the low-value tier; a loyalty programme for this tier is high-ROI
